MAE 322 Engineering Design Project: An Autonomous Search and Rescur Robot

This post showcases the completion of an autonomous search and rescue robot as part of my senior engineering design course. This was a 10 person collaborative project.

Spring 2025 David Herrera

Abstract:

The objective of this project is to design a Search and Rescue Robot that can autonomously traverse an obstacle course, navigate toward a light source, and deliver a medical kit. The first obstacle is a ramp with an overall length of 8 feet. The second obstacle is a dogleg chute, which is comprised of three 6-8 feet long segments, made of 18” high plywood walls, with a spacing of 3 feet. The final obstacle is a wall. The wall is 1 foot high and 3 feet wide, with a 6” step on the front side. A secondary goal of the project is to get the robot to traverse the course as fast as possible. The robot must also be robust enough to survive a 1 foot drop test.

The robot was designed to be a three-wheeled vehicle with an aluminum chassis. The wheels have 3D printed rubber hooks for better traction, and the robot is powered by a 12V battery and uses a DC CIM motor for propulsion. An Arduino Teensy microcontroller is used to control the motors and sensors, as well as run the autonomous script.

This project was completed in a group of 10 students, and proved to be an incredible teambuilding exercize. I was primarily responsible for the manufacturing of the robot, as well as the testing and validation of the autonomous code. Because it involved many machine shop hours, communication was essential in completing the project efficiently. Ultimately, the team chose to go with a "Perry The Platypus" Theme and 3D printed a cover for the robot to look like Perry!
